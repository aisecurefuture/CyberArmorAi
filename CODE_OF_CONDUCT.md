# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

CyberArmor is a professional, security-focused project maintained by Gratitech Research and Charitable Endeavor Corporation. Everyone who interacts with this project — including contributors, issue reporters, and community members — is expected to maintain a standard of conduct consistent with that professional context.

We are committed to making participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate professionally and respectfully
- Focus feedback on ideas, code, and technical substance — not people
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Be direct and honest; avoid ambiguity in technical discussions
- Respect that maintainers have final say on design and roadmap decisions
- Handle security-sensitive discussions with appropriate discretion (see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md))

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, personal attacks, or sustained disruption of discussions
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Discriminatory jokes, language, or imagery
- Deliberate intimidation or threatening behavior
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above

## Security-Specific Expectations

This is a security product. Contributors are expected to:

- Never introduce intentional backdoors, malicious code, or hidden functionality
- Report suspected vulnerabilities privately per [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) rather than disclosing them publicly in issues or pull requests
- Handle any sensitive data or credentials encountered in the course of contribution with care and discretion

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to **support@gratitech.com**. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and to ban contributors whose behavior is harmful or disruptive.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, code review) and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project.
